
## **1. Overview**

A **Security Operations Center (SOC)** is a centralized unit within an organization responsible for **monitoring, detecting, responding to, and mitigating cybersecurity threats**. It serves as the first line of defense against cyber attacks, ensuring the security, integrity, and availability of an organizationâ€™s information systems.

---

## **2. Key Functions of SOC**

- **Monitoring**: Continuous surveillance of network traffic, endpoints, and logs for suspicious activity.
    
- **Detection**: Identifying security incidents, anomalies, and potential threats using SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) tools.
    
- **Response**: Investigating alerts and responding to security incidents to prevent damage.
    
- **Analysis**: Conducting root cause analysis and threat intelligence research to understand threats.
    
- **Reporting**: Documenting incidents, trends, and metrics for management and compliance purposes.
    
- **Compliance**: Ensuring security policies, regulatory requirements, and best practices are followed.

## **3. Key Roles in SOC**

|**Role**|**Responsibilities**|
|---|---|
|**SOC Manager**|Oversees the SOC team, manages operations, strategy, and ensures compliance with security policies.|
|**Security Analyst (Tier 1)**|Monitors alerts, triages incidents, performs initial analysis, and escalates threats.|
|**Security Analyst (Tier 2)**|Conducts deeper investigation, performs forensic analysis, and coordinates incident response.|
|**Incident Responder / Threat Hunter**|Proactively searches for threats, analyzes breaches, and mitigates attacks.|
|**SOC Engineer / Architect**|Maintains SOC tools, configures SIEM, intrusion detection systems (IDS), and ensures infrastructure reliability.|
|**Threat Intelligence Analyst**|Gathers threat intelligence, tracks attack trends, and supports proactive defense measures.|

---

## **4. Tools Used in SOC**

- **SIEM Tools**: Splunk, QRadar, ArcSight
    
- **Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR)**: CrowdStrike, Carbon Black
    
- **Network Monitoring**: Wireshark, Zeek, Suricata
    
- **Threat Intelligence Platforms**: MISP, ThreatConnect
    

---

## **5. Best Practices**

- Maintain **24/7 monitoring** for early threat detection.
    
- Implement a **clear escalation process** for incidents.
    
- Regularly **update threat intelligence** to stay ahead of emerging threats.
    
- Conduct **post-incident reviews** to improve defenses.
    
- Ensure **cross-team collaboration** between IT, SOC, and management.
    

---

## **6. Summary**

The SOC acts as the **nerve center of cybersecurity operations**, combining people, processes, and technology to **detect, respond to, and mitigate cyber threats**. Understanding its roles and structure is essential for anyone pursuing a SOC Analyst career.
