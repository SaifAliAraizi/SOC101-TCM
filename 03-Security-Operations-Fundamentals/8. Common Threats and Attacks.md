### 1. **Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs)**

**Definition:**  
Long-term, targeted attacks by sophisticated threat actors, usually with political, financial, or espionage motives.  
**Characteristics:**

- Stealthy and persistent presence in target networks.
    
- Use multiple attack vectors (phishing, malware, exploits).
    
- Often state-sponsored or organized crime groups.
    

**Examples / References:**

- CrowdStrike APT Groups: [CrowdStrike APT](https://crowdstrike.com/adversaries/)
    
- Mandiant APT Insights: [Mandiant APT](https://mandiant.com/resources/insights/apt-groups)
    
- MITRE ATT&CK Groups: [MITRE APT Groups](https://attack.mitre.org/groups/)
    

---

### 2. **Malware Attacks**

**Definition:** Malicious software designed to damage or gain unauthorized access to systems.  
**Types:**

- Virus: Self-replicating code that spreads through files.
    
- Worm: Spreads automatically across networks.
    
- Trojan: Disguised as legitimate software.
    
- Ransomware: Encrypts data and demands ransom.
    
- Spyware / Keyloggers: Steals sensitive information.
    

---

### 3. **Phishing & Social Engineering**

**Definition:** Attacks targeting human weaknesses to steal credentials or deploy malware.  
**Types:**

- Email phishing: Fraudulent emails trick users into revealing information.
    
- Spear phishing: Targeted attack on specific individuals or organizations.
    
- Vishing / Smishing: Voice or SMS-based phishing attacks.
    

---

### 4. **Denial of Service (DoS) & Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)**

**Definition:** Attacks aimed at making a service or network unavailable by overwhelming it with traffic.  
**Types:**

- DoS: Single system floods target with requests.
    
- DDoS: Multiple systems (botnets) overwhelm the target simultaneously.
    

---

### 5. **Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attacks**

**Definition:** Intercepting and possibly altering communications between two parties without their knowledge.  
**Examples:**

- Eavesdropping on unencrypted network traffic.
    
- Session hijacking.
    

---

### 6. **SQL Injection & Web Application Attacks**

**Definition:** Exploiting vulnerabilities in web applications to access or manipulate databases.  
**Examples:**

- SQL Injection: Injecting malicious SQL queries.
    
- Cross-Site Scripting (XSS): Injecting malicious scripts into web pages.
    
- Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF): Forcing users to execute unwanted actions.
    

---

### 7. **Insider Threats**

**Definition:** Malicious or accidental actions by trusted personnel that compromise security.  
**Examples:**

- Data theft by employees.
    
- Misuse of credentials.
    
- Negligent behavior causing breaches.
    

---

### Key Points for SOC Analysts:

- Understand the **TTPs (Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures)** of threats.
    
- Use **MITRE ATT&CK framework** to map attacks and improve detection.
    
- Monitor for **indicators of compromise (IOCs)** and unusual behavior.
    
- Prioritize threats based on **impact, likelihood, and critical assets**.
